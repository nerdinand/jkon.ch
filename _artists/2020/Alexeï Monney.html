Throughout installations, sculptures, photography or texts, my work reconnects the human being with her*/himself. By flirting with imaginary censorship the artwork might provoke the public and push recipients out of their comfort zone - blurring the boundary between right and wrong. However, I do not expect a specific, but rather some sort of reaction, negative or positive, towards my artwork. In consequence, the reaction of the audience evolves. To sum up, my artwork can be described as a form of "social combat" which questions people about their way of life and their living together.
